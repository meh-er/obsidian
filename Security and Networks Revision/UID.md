#SaN 
A **User Identifier (UID)** is a unique **numeric** value assigned to each user account on a Unix/Linux system. UIDs are how the **kernel** identifies users and enforces access control. They are stored in the `**etc/passwd`** file.

Types of UID:  
- **Root UID:** The superuser (root) has a UID of `0`. This UID bypasses most permission checks.  
    
- **System UIDs**: Reserved for system services (e.g. `1` to `999`)
