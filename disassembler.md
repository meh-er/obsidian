#Theory 

A disassembler is a computer program that translates machine language into assembly language (opposite of an assembler). The output of disassembly is typically formatted for human-readability rather than for input to an assembler, making disassemblers a reverse engineering tool.

It differs from a decompiler, which targets a high level language rather than an assembly language. Unlike decompilers, disassemblers are aimed at generating a symbolic assembly, meaning it's attempting to reconstruct the assembly closest to its executions.
